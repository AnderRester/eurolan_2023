Destination

Alecu Russo Balti State University, Pushkin str., 38, Bălți, Republic of Moldova

Chisinau International Airport – Central Bus Station

In order to get from the Chisinau International Airport to Balti you can take a bus. You can choose between Central Bus Station and North Bus Station. Chisinau International Airport – Central Bus Station Chisinau International Airport is located 13 km away from the center of Chisinau city. To get from the airport to the Central Bus Station you can use the public transport:

  • Trolleybus No. 30

    It is on the route from 6:00 in the morning, until 23:00 in the evening. The fare is 2 MDL. The route is: Chisinau International Airport – blv. Dacia – viaduct – ave. Ciuflea – blv. Ştefan cel Mare şi Sfânt – ave. Mitropolit G. Bănulescu-Bodoni – ave. 31 august 1989
  • A taxi

Chisinau International Airport – North Bus Station (is preferred)

To get from the airport to the North Bus Station you can use the public transport:

  • Trolleybus No. 30

    Taxi minibus No. 165 It is on the route from 5:45 in the morning, until 22:00 in the evening. The fare is 6 MDL. The route is:
    Chisinau International Airport → Dacia Ave. → Trandafirilor Rd. → Decebal Ave. → Iu. Gagarin Ave. → C. Negruzzi Ave. → Ştefan cel Mare şi Sfînt Ave. → Ismail Rd.
  • A taxi

At the Central/North station, you may take a transit bus or minibus to Balti.

When you get the Balti Bus Station, you have to get out of the station and reach the public transport station, situated very close, at about 50 m. You can take any public transport that goes to the Center City (trolleybus (No. 1, 3), bus (No. 25) or minibus (No. 1, 8, 20)). You have to get off at the Central Station, situated on the Stefan cel Mare street and walk straight ahead until the intersection with Pushkin street and walk down to get the University (Pushkin 38 str.).

About

EUROLAN 2021 will be virtually hosted by two institutes of the Romanian Academy, the Research Institute for Artificial Intelligence in Bucharest and the Institute of Computer Science in Iași, as well as the “Alexandru Ioan Cuza” University of Iași. Tutorials coupled with hands-on sessions will be held throughout the week. The full schedule will be available on the school’s website soon. To continue the EUROLAN tradition over almost three decades, which are well-known for the excellence of their academic programs along with camaraderie among professors and students, we will arrange for a range of virtual activities in addition to the online classes aimed at providing cultural experiences and discoveries and interaction among participants.
